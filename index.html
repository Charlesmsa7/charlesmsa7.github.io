About Me

For references & testmonials about Charles CLICK HERE

I started my studies as a microbiologist, then turned to be a computational biologist.

In my doctoral research I have primarily focussed on structural evaluation, designing inhibitors and investigating the key prespectives involved in selective inhibition of G9a (EHMT2) lysine methyltransferse. By working with Dr.SMC, I gained extensive experiance in molecular dynamics and computer aided drug designing tools and strategies. During Ph.D., I have used molecular modelling and dynamics packages like GROMACS, AMBER, NAMD, Schoringer Maestro etc., A glimpse of my doctoral work can be found HERE.

Other than my doctoral research, I have contributed to many other research projects. To mention here, i). Identification of hotspot residues involved in the interaction of ASS1 with PRMT7 and Citrullinemia mutations that disrupt the interactions, ii). Mapping the interaction interface of HMT and RPL and identification of hotspot residues primarily involved in the interaction (unpublished).

With interest on next generation sequencing data analysis I have particiaped in B4 genomics workshop. Later, out of interest I have laearned few of NGS data such as RNAseq, ChipSeq and varient calling. For learning purposes, I tried to reproduce some published research data with different tools.

Currently looking for research positions to further learn more and improve my research career. Most interested to work with interdiciplinary research enviroiment.
