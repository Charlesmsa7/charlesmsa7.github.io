About Me

For references & testmonials about Charles CLICK HERE

I started my studies as a microbiologist, then turned to be a computational biologist.

In my doctoral research I have primarily focussed on structural evaluation, designing inhibitors and investigating the key perspectives involved in selective inhibition of G9a (EHMT2) lysine methyltransferse. By working with Dr.SMC, I gained extensive experience in molecular dynamics and computer aided drug designing tools and strategies. During Ph.D., I have used molecular modelling and dynamics packages like GROMACS, AMBER, NAMD, Schrodinger Maestro etc., Graphical summary of the thesis work is attached HERE .

Other than my doctoral research, I have contributed to many other research projects. To mention here, i). Identification of hotspot residues involved in the interaction of ASS1 with PRMT7 and Citrullinemia mutations that disrupt the interactions , ii). Mapping the interaction interface of HMT and RPL and identification of hotspot residues primarily involved in the interaction (unpublished). iii). Studying the role of Androgen receptor and Androgen binding protein in male contraception .

Other than biomolecular simulations, I have knowledge and minimal experience on next generation sequencing data analysis. I have participated in B4 genomics workshop which provided me the knowledge on NSG data analysis and library preparation. Later, out of interest I have worked on few, such as RNAseq, ChipSeq and variant calling. (github ).

Currently looking for research positions to further learn more and improve my research career. Most interested to work with inter-disciplinary research environment.
